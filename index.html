Optimizing Wastewater Treatment for Egypt's Water Future

A dynamic Decision-Support Tool that evaluates, diagnoses, and optimizes municipal wastewater treatment plants for safe agricultural reuse — reducing operational costs by up to 35% and recovering 92% of influent water.

01 — The Problem

Egypt's Growing Water Crisis

With per capita water availability plummeting from 2,000 m³/year in the 1960s to roughly 560 m³/year today, Egypt faces chronic water scarcity. Agriculture consumes 80% of freshwater while traditional irrigation loses 30–40% of water volume. By 2050, projected demand will reach 120 BCM — far outstripping current supply.

Treated wastewater offers a viable alternative for agricultural irrigation, but most existing plants fall short of the Egyptian Code ECP 501-2015 standards for unrestricted reuse. The gap between current effluent quality and regulatory targets demands a systematic, data-driven approach to plant evaluation and optimization.

02 — Methodology

How the DST Works

The tool integrates environmental engineering kinetics with multi-objective optimization to pinpoint bottlenecks and dynamically size hybrid treatment systems for Grade A compliance. Four core modules work in concert:

Σ

Water Quality Index

Aggregates multiple parameters into a single WQI score using AHP-weighted quality ratings against Egyptian Code ECP 501-2015. The composite index distills complex multi-parameter data into an actionable compliance snapshot.

Δ

Deviation Analysis

Computes the deviation vector D = P − S between plant readings and regulatory limits, with tolerance thresholds for measurement uncertainty. Parameters exceeding limits are flagged for targeted remediation.

λ

Reactor & UV Sizing

First-order decay kinetics for biological reactor sizing and Chick-Watson law for UV disinfection dosage — translated into actionable engineering specifications including volume, retention time, and lamp configuration.

⊕

MILP Optimization

Mixed-Integer Linear Programming balances CAPEX, OPEX, energy consumption, and nutrient recovery to find the optimal upgrade strategy from a combinatorial space of treatment configurations.

03 — Case Studies

Validated on Real Egyptian Plants

The DST was applied to two operational wastewater treatment plants in Egypt, demonstrating practical improvements under real-world conditions.

Station 1

Zenin WWTP

A secondary treatment plant with pathogen non-compliance as its primary issue. The DST recommended UV disinfection as the most cost-effective upgrade path, achieving a 4.4-log reduction in pathogen load.

  • Type: Secondary Treatment
  • Primary Issue: Pathogen Non-compliance
  • Solution: UV Disinfection
  • UV Dose: 29.8 mJ/cm²
  • Log Reduction: 4.4-log
Station 2

Abu Rawash WWTP

A primary treatment facility suffering from catastrophic multi-parameter failure. The DST prescribed a comprehensive upgrade: secondary biological treatment, UV disinfection, and biogas energy recovery.

  • Type: Primary Treatment
  • Primary Issue: Catastrophic Multi-param Failure
  • Solution: Secondary + UV + Biogas
  • BOD Removal: 96.8%
  • Energy Recovery: 768 MWh/day
